<p><p>The commercial dough conditioners market consists of two primary types: powders and fluids. Powdered dough conditioners are dry formulations that enhance dough properties, improve texture, and increase shelf life, making them convenient for storage and transport. Fluid dough conditioners, on the other hand, are liquid formulations that provide similar benefits but are often easier to mix into dough. Both types are crucial for bakeries and food manufacturers, optimizing the fermentation process and ensuring consistent product quality.</p></p>
